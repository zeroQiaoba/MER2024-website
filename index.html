MER24@IJCAI and MRAC24@ACM MM




Multimodal Emotion


Recognition




Noise Robustness, Semi-Supervised Learning, and Open-set Recognition


Multimodal emotion recognition is an active research topic in artificial intelligence. Its main goal is to integrate multi-modalities to identify human emotional states. Current works generally assume accurate emotion labels for benchmark datasets and focus on developing more effective architectures. However, existing technologies are difficult to meet the demand for practical applications. To this end, last year, we launched MER23@ACM Multimedia and MRAC23@ACM Multimedia. In this year, we will continuously holding related workshops and challenges that bring together researchers around the world to further discuss recent research and future directions for robust multimodal emotion recognition.

In this workshop and challenge, we aim to bring together researchers from the fields of multimodal modeling of human affect, modality robustness of affect recognition, low-resource affect recognition, human affect synthesis in multimedia, privacy in affective computing, and applications in health, education, entertainment, etc., to further discuss recent research and future directions for affective computing in multimedia. At the same time, we intend to provide a communication platform for all participants of MER24, to systematically evaluate the robustness of emotion recognition systems and promote applications of this technology in practice.

MER24 Challenge@IJCAI

Compared with previous MER23, in this year, we enlarge the dataset size by including more labeled and unlabeled samples. Meanwhile, besides MER-NOISE and MER-SEMI, we introduce a new track called MER-Openset.


Track 1. MER-Noise. This track provides corrupted samples to evaluate modality robustness. Specifically, we blur video frames and add noise to the audio in this track. We encourage participants to exploit data augmentation [1] or other advanced techniques [2] to improve the noise robustness.

Track 2. MER-SEMI. Previously, MERBench [3] proved that pre-training on large amounts of unlabeled data (especially pre-training on domain-matching data) was effective than other techniques for emotion recognition. Therefore, this track provides a large number of unlabeled samples belonging to the same domain as the labeled data. We encourage participants to exploit semi-supervised learning to improve the performance of emotion recognition.

Track 3. MER-Openset. The first two tracks fix the label space. Differently, this track focuses on open-set emotion recognition. We encourage participants to leverage the recent MLLMs to generate fine-grained emotion labels. Our initial attemp to this task can be found in EMER [4].


Evaluation Metrics. For Track 1 and Track 2, the goal is to predict discrete emotions. We choose the weighted average f1-score as the evaluation metric. The goal of Track 3 is to predict open-set emotion labels. For this track, we calculate the accuracy and recall between annotated and predicted open-set labels, and use the average of these two metrics for the final ranking. More details can be found in EMER.


Dataset. Please download the End User License Agreement, fill it out and send it to merchallenge.contact@gmail.com to access the data. We will review your application and get in touch as soon as possible. EULA requires participants to use this dataset only for academic research and not to edit or upload samples to the Internet. Permission is granted to use the data given that you agree: (1) That you include a reference to the MER24 Dataset in any work that makes use of the dataset. (2) That you do not distribute this dataset or modified versions. (3) That you may not use the dataset or any derivative work for commercial purposes as, for example, licensing or selling the data, or using the data with a purpose to procure a commercial gain. (4) That all rights not expressly granted to you are reserved by MER24 organizers.


Result submission. We provide a large amount of unlabeled samples. For Track 1 and Track 2, participants should submit discrete prediction for each unlabeled samples. For Track 3, participants should submit open-set emotion prediction for each unlabeled samples. During evaluation, we only evaluate a small subset of samples. To focus on generalization performance rather than optimizing for a specific subset, we do not provide information about which samples belong to the test subset.

MRAC24 Workshop@ACM MM



Besides papers for MER24 Challenge, we also invite submissions on any aspect of multimodal emotion recognition and synthesis in deep learning. Topics include but not limited to:

  • Multimodal modeling of human affect
  • Modality robustness of affect recognition
  • LLM-based and MLLM-based emotion recognition
  • Open-set emotion recognition
  • Low-resource affect recognition
  • Contextualized modeling of affective states
  • Human affect synthesis in multimedia
  • Privacy in affective computing
  • Explainable, fair, trustworthy in affective computing
  • Applications in health, education, entertainment, etc.
